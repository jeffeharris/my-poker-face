# The Circuit — Famous Hands Library

Real, documented hands sourced from the internet (not invented) to anchor the
career-mode lessons. Built from a design riff (2026-05-31) with three hard
constraints from Jeff:

1. **Use real hands** — don't fabricate scenarios; pull famous/documented ones.
2. **Solid lessons, not blind luck** — a hand earns a `SKILL` tag only if the
   *decision* is the point. Suck-outs, coolers, and bad beats are `LORE` (flavor
   or cautionary) and must NOT be used as teaching spots.
3. **Scene 0 opponent is a FISH** — Loose Larry over-bluffs busted draws, calls
   too much, and only bets big when he actually has it. So the famous **pro-vs-pro**
   reads (folding the second nuts on a soul-read, balanced bluff wars) are
   **later-arc** material for tougher rooms up the stakes ladder — they don't fit
   a fish tutorial. The fish lessons are louder and simpler.

A recurring framing that fell out of this: **the hero does what the legend got
wrong.** Farha *folded* the best hand to a bluff → you call. Seidel *couldn't
fold* top pair into the trap → you lay it down. Sal is teaching you the hands the
greats misplayed.

Legend: **Tier** = `FISH` (loud, exploitable — Scene 0 / early circuit) ·
`REG/PRO` (balanced, needs reads — later arc). **Class** = `SKILL` (a real
decision) · `LORE` (luck/cooler/flavor — never a graded lesson).

## SKILL · FISH — Scene-0 / early-circuit lessons

### 1. Bluff-catch — Moneymaker vs Farha (2003 WSOP Main Event)
- **Hero seat (you):** Sammy Farha — **Q♠ 9♥** (top pair, pair of nines)
- **Villain (the fish/Larry):** Chris Moneymaker — **K♠ 7♥** (missed every draw; king-high air)
- **Board:** `9♠ 2♦ 6♠` · turn · `… 3♥` river — ***suits disputed across retellings***
  (the popular "turn 8♠" version gives Moneymaker a made flush, which contradicts
  "missed all draws / king-high." Reliable facts: Farha flopped top pair; Moneymaker
  barreled busted draws into a king-high air; Farha **folded the best hand** to the
  river all-in.)
- **Lesson:** A fish barrels his busted draws to the felt. When he keeps firing and
  you hold a real piece, **look him up** — don't let air bluff you off the best hand.
  *Pass = not folded.*
- **Why it fits a fish:** over-bluffing is the fish's loudest leak. (See
  `reference_fish_is_tiered_weak_fish` — the live `weak_fish` over_bluffs.)
- Source: [Upswing](https://upswingpoker.com/chris-moneymaker-vs-sammy-farha/) ·
  [888poker](https://www.888poker.com/magazine/poker-world/7-famous-poker-hands-in-history)

### 2. Hero call (the call that works) — Mercier vs Koskas (EPT4 San Remo)
- **Hero (you):** Jason Mercier — **9♠ 5♥** (a *pair of fives*, bottom-ish pair)
- **Villain (the fish/Larry):** Eric Koskas — **T♥ 3♠** (total air; jammed when the board paired)
- **Board:** `5♣ J♥ 6♦ 8♣ 8♥` (river pairs the 8; Koskas insta-shoves)
- **Lesson:** When a wild player suddenly **jams an all-in on a scary runout**,
  it's often the busted hand trying to buy it. A modest made hand can be a
  **hero call**. *Pass = not folded.* Companion to #1 — Farha folded and was the
  goat; Mercier called and was the hero.
- **Why it fits a fish:** a fish over-jams air far more than a balanced reg.
- Source: [PokerNews](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2018/12/poker-moments-mercier-hero-calls-in-san-remo-32962.htm) ·
  [PokerTube](https://www.pokertube.com/article/sickest-hero-calls)

### 3. Discipline / the trap — Chan vs Seidel (1988 WSOP Main Event)
- **Hero seat (you):** Erik Seidel — **Q♣ 7♣** (top pair, queens)
- **Villain (the fish/Larry):** Johnny Chan — **J♥ 9♠** (flopped the nut straight)
- **Board:** `Q♦ 8♠ 10♣` (flop) · `2♠` (turn) · `6` (river) — Chan checks the
  turn to set the trap; Seidel jams the river into the made straight.
- **Lesson:** Even a passive fish catches a card. When the quiet guy **suddenly
  commits big out of nowhere, believe him** — fold your one-pair hand. The hand
  Seidel *couldn't* get away from; you can. *Pass = **folded**.*
- **Doubles as:** the **slow-play / value** lesson from Chan's side for a later
  beat — check the monster to let the station hang himself.
- Source: [PokerNews](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2019/03/poker-moments-erik-seidel-johnny-chan-eye-to-the-sky-33438.htm) ·
  [PokerListings](https://www.pokerlistings.com/news/wsop-main-event-heads-up-flashback-erik-seidel-vs-johnny-chan-54663)

### 4. Value / make the station pay — slow-played set of sevens ($5/$10 cash)
- **Hero (you):** flopped **set of sevens** on `K♠ 7♦ 2♣` (dry rainbow)
- **Villain (the fish/Larry):** a top-pair/overpair calling station that keeps barreling
- **Line:** check-call the flop to keep his bluffs/value in, then take three streets
  (he can't fold top pair to your river raise).
- **Lesson:** Against a station, **don't bluff — extract.** A fish pays off top pair
  forever, so bet/raise your strong hands relentlessly. *Pass = not folded (he pays you).*
- **Note:** documented teaching hand (Red Chip "streets of value"), not a *named*
  famous hand — value-betting a fish doesn't make ESPN, which is itself the point.
- Source: [Red Chip Poker](https://redchippoker.com/streets-of-value-poker-podcast/)

## SKILL · REG/PRO — later-arc lessons (tougher rooms, NOT the fish tutorial)

### 5. The great laydown — Robl vs Gavri (High Stakes Poker Live, 2026)
- **Folder (the read):** Andrew Robl — **Q♥ 7♥** (queen-high flush = the *second* nuts)
- **Villain:** Justin Gavri — **K♥ 9♥** (king-high flush = the stone nuts)
- **Board:** `10♣ 2♥ A♥` (flop) · `9♠` (turn) · `6♥` (river)
- **Action:** Robl bets $125k into $96k; Gavri jams $290k; Robl tanks 3 min and
  **folds the second nuts** — correctly.
- **Lesson:** Against a *thinking* player, a massive river jam into a made-flush
  board can beat even a monster. The top of your range is still foldable. **A fish
  could never make this bet or this fold** — strictly later-arc.
- Source: [PokerNews](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2026/04/andrew-robl-makes-big-high-stakes-poker-fold-50973.htm)

### 6. Bluff war / fold equity — Ivey vs Paul Jackson (Monte Carlo Millions)
- Phil Ivey bluff-raises with **queen-high** into Jackson's own bluff and takes it.
- **Lesson:** vs a balanced opponent, aggression and credible lines manufacture
  fold equity — but only when the story adds up. (Exact board not reliably
  documented; keep as a *concept* beat, not a rigged spot.)
- Source: [Ivey MasterClass coverage](https://www.tightpoker.com/phil-iveys-greatest-hands-a-masterclass-in-poker-strategy/)

teaching spot.

## How Scene 0 uses this (the fish tutorial)

Scene 0 seeds **three** SKILL·FISH hands, in this order, cast with the hero in the
seat the legend misplayed and **Larry (the fish) in the aggressor/loser seat**:

1. **Value** (#4) — Larry calls everything; bet for value, make him pay.
2. **Bluff-catch** (#1, optionally echoed by #2) — Larry barrels busted air; call.
3. **Discipline** (#3) — Larry the passive fish wakes up with the goods; fold.

The boards/matchups come from the real hands above; the "who's the fish" casting
is the game's. Pro-vs-pro hands (#5, #6) are held back for the climb into tougher
rooms. The LORE pile feeds flavor, names, and cautionary one-liners — never a
graded spot.
