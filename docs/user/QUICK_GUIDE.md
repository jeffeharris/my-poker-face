# My Poker Face — Quick Guide for New Players

Poker against AI that feels human. This guide covers what you need to know to sit down and play your first session.

> **Heads up**: this guide assumes you know the basics of Texas Hold'em (hand rankings, blinds, betting rounds). The game doesn't teach the rules of poker — only how to play *this* game.

## 1. Getting In

From the landing page, click **Play Now**, then choose:

- **Guest** — instant play, but limited to **1 active game** and **10 hands** before you hit a usage cap.
- **Named account** — keeps your game history, career stats, and the AI's memory of you across sessions. Recommended.

## 2. Starting a Game

From the **Game Menu**, you have five options:

| Option | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Quick Play** | Pick 2–6 opponents; personalities are chosen for you |
| **Custom Game** | Hand-pick personalities, set blinds, starting stack, blind growth |
| **Themed Game** | Curated personality bundles (Science Masters, Hollywood Legends, Sports Champions, etc.) |
| **Continue Game** | Resume a paused tournament |
| **Cash Mode** | Multi-table lobby with persistent bankroll |

### Game Mode (difficulty)

Choose at creation. Cannot be changed mid-game.

- **Casual** — personality-driven, easy-going
- **Standard** — balanced, some GTO awareness
- **Competitive** — strong GTO play with trash talk
- **Pro** — full GTO, analytical

**Recommended first run**: Quick Play → 3 opponents → Casual.

## 3. The Table

What's on screen during a hand:

- **Top bar** — game name, blinds, pot, hand number, your stack
- **Center** — circular table, community cards, avatars, dealer button, action indicators
- **Bottom** — your hole cards, position (BTN / SB / BB / UTG), action buttons
- **Right panel — Activity Feed** — running log of every action and chat message; the text box at the bottom is where you talk

### Your Actions

Buttons appear only when valid for the situation:

- **Fold** — give up the hand
- **Check** — pass the action (no bet to call)
- **Call** — match the current bet
- **Bet / Raise** — use the slider or type an amount

There is **no hand-strength meter, no pot odds, no equity %**. The math is on you.

## 4. Talking to the AI

This is the heart of the game.

- **Quick Chat Suggestions** (collapsible panel) — AI-written one-liners in six tones: **Tilt, Bait, Needle, Goad, Bluff, Befriend**. You can target a specific opponent and adjust intensity.
- AI opponents reply in character — Lincoln, Churchill, Gordon Ramsay, Batman, and others. Expect dramatic stage directions like `*narrows eyes*` or `*pushes chips forward slowly*`.
- AIs **remember you**. Past hands, beats, and grudges resurface in later sessions.

## 5. Reading Opponents

You will **not** see labels like "tight-aggressive." Personality is something you discover by watching. What you *can* see:

- Names and avatars
- Bet sizing and timing patterns
- Chat — leaks playing style and emotional state
- **Career Stats** highlights your top **nemesis** (who eliminates you most)

Everything else — the psychology system, tilt tracking, range guidance — runs invisibly. The game is built for drama, not dashboards.

## 6. Tournament Flow

- Blinds rise on a schedule
- You're knocked out when your stack hits zero
- Game ends when one player remains
- **Auto-saves after every action** — close the tab and come back to **Continue Game**

## 7. Cash Mode

- Pick a stake level, sit at a table (4 AI + up to 2 humans)
- Stand and switch tables freely
- **Sponsor loans**: if your stack runs low, you can borrow chips from an AI lender at interest. Settles when you leave the table.
- Your bankroll persists across sessions

## 8. Stats & Progression

The **Stats** page tracks games played, win rate, total knockouts, your last result, and your current nemesis. There's no XP, unlocks, or seasons — just a record of who you've beaten and who's beaten you.

## 9. What This Game Doesn't Do

So you know what to expect:

- No rules tutorial — learn Hold'em elsewhere first
- No strategy hints, equity displays, or in-game coach
- No human-vs-human multiplayer — you're playing AI
- Browser only; no mobile app

## 10. First-Session Checklist

1. Register a **named account** (don't burn your run as a guest)
2. Start a **Quick Play** game with **3 opponents** in **Casual** mode
3. Open the **Quick Chat** panel, pick a tone, target one opponent, watch the reaction
4. Play one full tournament to feel the blind structure escalate
5. Check the **Stats** page — meet your first nemesis

## Common Beginner Mistakes

- **Calling too much** — paying to "see what they have" is the fastest way to bleed chips
- **Playing every hand** — fold most starting hands, especially from early position
- **Bluffing into multiple opponents** — bluffs work against one player, not three
- **Taking AI chat at face value** — they bluff with words too
- **Tilting after a bad beat** — variance is real; one hand isn't the session

## Where to Go Next

- Try a **Themed Game** to meet a new cast
- Switch to **Competitive** mode once Casual feels easy
- Try **Cash Mode** if you prefer drop-in/drop-out play over tournaments
